What is a Data Center?

Think of it as essential infrastructure‚Äîlike a power plant or water treatment facility‚Äîthat keeps hospitals, schools, local businesses, government agencies, and everyday cloud services operating reliably. There are no retail storefronts, no stadium-level crowds, no constant truck traffic. Just secure, climate-controlled spaces with computer equipment, advanced cooling systems, and clean power delivery.

Server Infrastructure

Rows of servers and networking equipment processing the cloud computing workloads that modern life depends on‚Äîfrom business applications to healthcare systems to online education.

Advanced Cooling Systems

State-of-the-art closed-loop air-cooled systems that maintain optimal operating temperatures while consuming virtually no water under normal conditions. Heat is rejected to ambient air via condenser fans.

Backup Power Systems

Generators that ensure uninterrupted operations during utility outages. These are regulated and run briefly for mandatory testing and during rare power disruptions‚Äîaveraging approximately 20 hours per year total across all units.

Security & Monitoring

24/7/365 professional security and sophisticated monitoring systems ensuring continuous, reliable operations with minimal staffing requirements and vehicle traffic.

Frequently Asked Questions

These questions come directly from Decatur Township residents, neighbors, and local partners. If you don't see your question answered here, please email us and we'll add it.

Will my electric or water utility bills increase because of this project?+
No. Sabey pays 100% of all customer-specific infrastructure, dedicated facilities, and applicable utility riders required for this project. Sabey also pays CIAC (Contributions in Aid of Construction) for any upstream reinforcements attributable to this load. New electrical load actually adds utility revenue that helps spread fixed infrastructure costs across a larger customer base, which can help stabilize rates for all customers. Any broader grid programs would go through IURC (Indiana Utility Regulatory Commission) review for public oversight. The closed-loop cooling system means virtually no water consumption during normal operations. See the official AES Will-to-Serve Letter and Citizens Water Will-to-Serve Letter for details.
How much water will the data center actually use?+
The facility design utilizes closed-loop air-cooled systems, meaning virtually zero water consumption during normal operations. Unlike traditional data centers that use evaporative cooling towers, our system recirculates water in a closed loop while rejecting heat to ambient air via condenser fans‚Äînot drawing from municipal water supplies or local aquifers. Small amounts of water are used for domestic needs like restrooms and break rooms, but cooling operations consume virtually no water. This design choice is specifically tailored for the Indianapolis project to ensure local efficiency and environmental sustainability. Reference: Air-Cooled Chiller Technical Documentation and Citizens Water Will-to-Serve Letter.
What about noise from cooling equipment and backup generators?+
All mechanical equipment is fully enclosed within buildings and acoustically treated to minimize sound transmission. Exterior lighting is professionally shielded to eliminate glare and light pollution. Backup generators are regulated by local ordinances and operate only during brief mandatory testing periods and rare utility outages‚Äîtypically totaling approximately 20 hours per year across all units. Testing is scheduled during reasonable hours with advance notice to neighbors. Normal daily operations are designed to be significantly quieter than typical industrial or manufacturing facilities.
Will this increase traffic congestion in our neighborhood?+
No. Data center daily operations are inherently low-traffic compared to other industrial uses. The facility will employ 50-75 people working in shifts, meaning 15-25 employees arriving and departing at any given time.
