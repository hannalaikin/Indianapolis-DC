What this means for you

Your bill

Your power bill will not go up because of this project

We pay our fair share. Service will be under a cost-of-service tariff or a Commission-approved customer-specific contract. The project pays all applicable riders and customer-specific facilities so costs aren’t shifted to neighbors.

Low water usage

For Indianapolis, our closed-loop design uses virtually no water during normal operation. Where water is used elsewhere, recycled sources are often leveraged.

Quiet by design

Cooling equipment and generators are enclosed and acoustically treated. Generator run time is limited (testing and true outages) and facilities meet or exceed local noise standards.

Benefits for Decatur Township

Major investment

$4B+ over time. That scale helps anchor the local tax base and supports essential services.

Better roads

We’re committing funding for township road improvements tied to the project. Final segments will be coordinated with local partners and shared publicly.

Jobs & training

High-skill, clean roles in operations; long-term work for electricians, HVAC, and local suppliers. We’ll partner with schools and community colleges to grow local talent.

Environment & design

Stormwater

Engineered retention, bioswales, and permeable surfaces manage runoff on site and protect local waterways.

Lighting & visuals

Shielded lighting, landscape screening, and materials that respect surrounding uses reduce visual impact.

Efficient power use

Purpose-built efficiency and strong PUE performance; our facilities routinely earn top ENERGY STAR scores.

Traffic & transportation

Independent analysis shows the current plan generates about 80% fewer peak-hour trips than the previously approved industrial plan. Operations have low daily trips; construction traffic is scheduled and managed with the city.

Frequently asked questions

Short, plain-English answers. Download the full FAQ for more detail.

Download Full FAQ (PDF)
▶ Will my power bill go up?
No. The project pays its own customer-specific costs under regulated agreements. New load also adds revenue that helps spread fixed costs.
▶ How much water will this use?
The Indianapolis design is closed-loop, which means virtually no water during normal operation. Cooling choices are made to fit local conditions and sustainability goals.
▶ What about noise?
Equipment is enclosed and acoustically treated. We adhere to local noise standards; generators run briefly for testing and in rare outages.
▶ Traffic and trucks in neighborhoods?
Daily trips are low. Access is planned to keep trucks on designated routes. The traffic study shows ~80% fewer peak trips than the previously approved use.
▶ What do we get as a community?
$4B+ investment over time, new tax revenues that support schools, roads, and public safety, road improvements we help fund, and local jobs with training pathways.
