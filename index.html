What’s is a data center?

Think of it as reliable, efficient infrastructure that keeps hospitals, schools, local businesses, and public agencies online. There are no retail storefronts, no stadium-level crowds — just secure rooms with computers, advanced cooling, and clean power systems.

  • • Built for efficiency and uptime, not crowds
  • • Secure, sealed, temperature-controlled rooms
  • • Quiet operations with limited daily staff
  • • Designed to minimize neighborhood impacts

Our plan emphasizes closed-loop cooling (virtually no water in normal operation), shielded lighting, landscaped buffers, and engineered stormwater systems to protect local waterways.

Our Story

Who is Sabey Data Centers?

For over 25 years, Sabey has designed, built, and operated highly efficient data centers across the U.S. With six active campuses and 3.5M+ square feet delivered, Sabey is known for reliability, environmental stewardship, and long-term community partnerships.

Every project starts the same way: transparency, collaboration with local leaders, and a design that keeps neighborhood impacts low while growing opportunity.

Why Indiana and why Decatur Township?

Indiana’s stable business climate, strong utility partnerships, and skilled workforce make it a natural home for technology infrastructure. Decatur Township offers logistics access, community leadership, and room to create a thoughtfully buffered campus that fits its surroundings.

This project helps anchor the local tax base for decades while creating high-skill jobs and supplier opportunities, without shifting utility costs to neighbors.

Frequently asked questions

These questions came directly from neighbors and local partners. If you don’t see yours, email us — we’ll add it.

Will my utility bill increase?+
No. Sabey covers 100% of customer-specific and dedicated facilities and pays all applicable riders. New loads add utility revenue that helps spread fixed costs. See the AES Will-to-Serve Letter.
How much water will the facility use?+
The design is closed-loop, meaning virtually no water during normal operation. Cooling choices are selected for local efficiency and sustainability. Reference: Air-Cooled Chiller.
What about noise and generators?+
Equipment is enclosed and acoustically treated; exterior lighting is shielded. Generators run briefly for testing and during rare utility outages, typically ~20 hours per year total.
Will traffic increase in our neighborhood?+
Daily operations are low-traffic with designated truck routes. Independent study shows ~80% fewer peak-hour trips than a previously approved industrial plan. See the Traffic Study.
How does this help Decatur Township?+
Significant long-term capital investment (~$4B+) strengthens the tax base that funds schools, roads, and public safety. We’re also committing funds to road improvements and partnering with schools on workforce programs.
Who can I talk to if I have concerns?+
We welcome every question. Email our community liaison at hannalaikin@gmail.com. We aim to reply within two business days.

Talk with us

Questions, ideas, or concerns — we want to hear them. We live with the results of this project together, and we’re committed to doing this right.
