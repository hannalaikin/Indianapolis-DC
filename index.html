About the Project

Project Background

The Dataland project is an ambitious initiative to build a state-of-the-art data center campus in Indiana.

Community Questions Answered

Will this be noisy?

No. All cooling equipment is enclosed within buildings with acoustic barriers. We meet or exceed local noise ordinances and conduct equipment testing only during daytime hours with advance community notice.

Learn about our facility design →

How much water will you use?

Our facilities use closed-loop or air-based cooling systems, consuming water comparable to office buildings. Many locations use "free cooling" most of the year, drastically reducing water needs.

See Seattle's efficiency →

What about traffic impact?

Once operational, fewer than 50 employees work daily shifts. Construction traffic is managed with planned routing and timing to minimize disruption to local roads.

Are these jobs really good jobs?

Yes. Data center positions average $75k-$100k annually, plus benefits. These include roles in engineering, security, maintenance, and IT operations - all requiring local talent and training.

View career opportunities →

Will you actually stay committed long-term?

Absolutely. Our partner has 20+ years in the industry with zero facility closures. Data centers are 20+ year investments requiring ongoing community partnership.

See our track record →

How will this affect property values?

Data centers typically enhance property values through job creation, tax revenue, and infrastructure improvements, while maintaining community character through thoughtful design.

Timeline

Construction is planned over multiple phases, with groundbreaking in 2025 and full operations expected by 2028.
