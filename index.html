Frequently Asked Questions

Where is the project site located?

The campus is located at the corner of Kentucky Avenue & Camby Road on a 130-acre site that was already zoned for light industrial use. Our data center will generate significantly less commercial and employee traffic than the previously planned industrial warehouse park.

Will this be noisy?

No. All cooling equipment is enclosed within buildings with acoustic barriers. We meet or exceed local noise ordinances and conduct equipment testing only during daytime hours with advance community notice.

How much water will you use?

Our facilities use closed-loop or air-based cooling systems, consuming water comparable to office buildings. Many locations use “free cooling” most of the year, drastically reducing water needs【407554511351221†L16-L27】.

What about traffic impact?

Once operational, fewer than 50 employees work daily shifts. Construction traffic is managed with planned routing and timing to minimize disruption to local roads.

Will electric bills go up?

No. Sabey’s energy-efficient design and use of renewable energy ensure stable energy usage that does not increase local electric rates【345476460734987†L85-L96】.

Does Sabey pay its fair share?

Yes. Data centers make significant contributions to local budgets through property taxes. For example, data centers in Quincy, WA account for about 75% of the city’s property tax revenue, funding schools and public services【462808654766062†L93-L97】.
