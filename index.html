About the Project

Project Background

The Dataland project is an ambitious initiative to build a state-of-the-art data center campus in Indiana, driving economic growth and technological innovation.

Timeline

Construction is planned over multiple phases, with groundbreaking in 2025 and full operations expected by 2028.

How It Works

Data centers are secure, energy-efficient facilities housing servers and networking equipment that store and process digital information. They enable cloud computing, AI, and essential services.

Economic Benefits

The campus will generate thousands of jobs, inject billions in local investment, and diversify Indiana’s economy.

Sustainability Commitments

We’re committed to renewable energy, efficient cooling, and minimal environmental impact, aligning with Indiana’s sustainability goals.

Partnerships

Dataland collaborates with local utilities, technology providers, and community organizations to ensure success.

Workforce Opportunities

We’ll offer training, apprenticeships, and career pathways in technology and construction for local talent.

When will the data center be operational?

What sustainability measures are being used?

We’ll employ renewable energy, efficient cooling, noise abatement, and minimal water use to minimize our environmental footprint.
