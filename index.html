What economic benefits will this project provide?

This project will create more than 50 permanent jobs averaging $75k–$100k annually, along with about 1,000 construction jobs during the build phase and 4.9–7.1 additional indirect jobs per direct job. How much water will you use?

Our facilities rely on closed‑loop or air‑based cooling systems that recirculate water, using amounts comparable to a typical office building. Many locations leverage “free cooling” most of the year, delivering low water usage and significant conservation【6728303114981†L165-L167】.

Where is the project site located?

The campus is located at the corner of Kentucky Avenue & Camby Road on a 130‑acre site that was already zoned for light industrial use. Our data center campus will generate significantly less commercial and employee traffic than the previously planned industrial warehouse park.

Will there be noise or traffic?

No. All cooling equipment is enclosed within buildings with acoustic barriers, and we meet or exceed local noise ordinances. Once operational, fewer than 50 employees work daily shifts, and construction traffic is managed to minimize disruption to local roads.

Does Sabey pay its fair share?

Yes. Sabey’s data centers make substantial contributions to local budgets through property taxes. For example, data centers in Quincy, WA account for about 75% of the city’s property tax revenue【6728303114981†L185-L187】, funding schools, hospitals, and community services.
