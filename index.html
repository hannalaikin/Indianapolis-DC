Environmental design & zoning

Land use & zoning

Data centers are commonly classified as light or high-tech industrial and often permitted in those districts. We coordinate with planning staff early to ensure compatibility.

Water & cooling

Closed-loop cooling for Indianapolis means virtually no water during normal operation. Where water is used in other markets, operators often leverage recycled or reclaimed sources.

Stormwater

Engineered retention, bioswales, and permeable surfaces manage runoff on-site and protect local waterways.

Noise & lighting

Facilities are designed to meet or exceed local noise ordinances. Exterior lighting is shielded to reduce light spill; landscape buffers and screening reduce visual impact.

Backup power

Emergency generators are highly regulated and used sparingly — typically ~20 hours per year for testing and rare outages.

Energy efficiency

Sabey’s purpose-built design prioritizes low overhead energy use and continuous improvement of PUE, with top-tier ENERGY STAR performance.

Case Study: Quincy, Washington

A real example of how Sabey Data Centers strengthens local economies and invests in long-term partnerships.

From Infrastructure to Impact

In Quincy, WA, Sabey’s data-center campus became a cornerstone of the local tax base. New revenues helped fund a new high school with a performing-arts center, a new city hall, and a regional medical facility — all while keeping community power rates stable.

Partnership in Practice

Sabey collaborated with the Quincy School District and community colleges to establish Career and Technical Education (CTE) programs preparing students for jobs in technology, HVAC, electrical, and construction trades.

The Quincy project serves as a model for how Sabey approaches Decatur Township — as a long-term community partner, not just a developer.
